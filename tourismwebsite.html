OLUMO ROCK

Olumo Rock Tourist Complex is a premier destination that blends natural wonder, history, and modern attractions. Nestled in Ikija, Abeokuta, Ogun State, near the famous Itoku Adire Market, the Complex offers an unforgettable experience of adventure, culture, and relaxation.

ILÉ ỌRỌ́

Ile Oro is a Yoruba translation for "house of wealth," and in other translations, “house of riches.” Every time culture is a subject of discussion, we all try to express how rich our individual culture is. The truth is, words alone can't express how rich any culture is, and I was tired of talking. As an African (Yoruba) architectural designer, I preferred to express the richness of my culture with a statement noticeable from miles away. One that speaks the native language… a cultural landmark that beats the test of time.

JOHN RANDLE CENTRE FOR YORUBA CULTURE & HISTORY

As an all-encompassing celebration of Yoruba art, fashion, literature, music, and history, the John Randle Centre’s collections have been drawn from the communities they celebrate. RAA provided both exhibition design and project management work on the John Randle Centre. For this, we worked under Seun Oduwole of SI.SA Architecture, who designed the building and assembled the curatorial, exhibition, and installation teams to execute his vision for the Centre.

NIKE, ART GALLERY

The Nike Art Gallery is a globally renowned cultural institution and a vibrant hub for Nigerian art, celebrating the nation's rich heritage and creative brilliance. The Nike Art Gallery is arguably the largest art gallery in West Africa, serving as a premier cultural hub for Nigerian and African art. Founded by world-renowned artist and philanthropist Chief Nike Davies-Okundaye (popularly known as "Mama Nike"), the gallery is dedicated to preserving and promoting Nigeria's rich artistic heritage.
